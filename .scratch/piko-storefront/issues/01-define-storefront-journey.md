# Define the Storefront Journey

Type: `grilling`
Status: `resolved`
Blocked by:

## Question

What sequence of pages, sections, and customer actions should take the Target Customer from first impression through product selection to completion of the clearly marked demo checkout?

## Answer

Use one continuous Bulgarian storefront rather than separate catalog and product pages:

1. Open with the PIKO identity, a concise value proposition, an animated cheese object, and a primary `Избери сирене` action that scrolls to product comparison.
2. Compare Regular Cheese and Crystal Cheese side by side with price, concise differentiators, quantity controls, and `Добави`. Each product is limited to 10 demo units.
3. Keep a persistent cart control visible. Adding an item updates its quantity and total immediately and gives brief, non-blocking visual confirmation.
4. Explain Sliven-only next-day delivery, the EUR 2 fee, free delivery from EUR 20, and the absence of a minimum order.
5. Open an editable cart drawer with quantity and remove controls, subtotal, delivery fee, total, and progress toward free delivery.
6. Use one focused demo-checkout step for name, Bulgarian phone number, Sliven address, delivery window, and an optional note.
7. Show a review summary and an explicit notice that no real order will be submitted.
8. Simulate completion with a demo order reference and a return-to-store action.

Navigation contains the PIKO mark, product and delivery anchors, and the cart. The prototype has no account, search, categories, or separate product pages. It prevents progress with an empty cart, invalid contact details, unavailable quantities, or missing delivery information, and preserves entered data when moving backward.
