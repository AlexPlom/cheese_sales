# Define the Storefront Journey

Type: `grilling`
Status: `resolved`
Blocked by:

## Question

What sequence of pages, sections, and customer actions should take the Target Customer from first impression through product selection to completion of the clearly marked demo checkout?

## Answer

Use one continuous Bulgarian storefront rather than separate catalog and product pages:

1. Open with the CURD. identity, a concise value proposition, an animated cheese object, and a primary `Избери сирене` action that scrolls to the catalog.
2. Present familiar cheeses with price, concise characteristics, quantity controls, and `Добави`. Each product is limited to 10 demo units.
3. Keep a persistent cart control visible. Adding an item updates its quantity and total immediately and gives brief, non-blocking visual confirmation.
4. Explain chilled delivery in one to two days, the EUR 2 fee, free delivery from EUR 25, and the absence of a minimum order.
5. Open an editable cart drawer with quantity and remove controls, subtotal, delivery fee, total, and progress toward free delivery.
6. Use one focused demo-checkout step for name, Bulgarian phone number, delivery address, delivery window, and an optional note.
7. Show a review summary and an explicit notice that no real order will be submitted.
8. Simulate completion with a demo order reference and a return-to-store action.

Navigation contains the CURD. mark, product and delivery anchors, and the cart. The prototype has no account, search, categories, or separate product pages. It prevents progress with an empty cart, invalid contact details, unavailable quantities, or missing delivery information, and preserves entered data when moving backward.
